Release of S106 Funds - We are Making Some Progress

August 3, 2020

We want to thank our District Councillors, Stephen Hirst, Nikki Ind and Richard Norris for organising a meeting today between representatives from the Dolphins Recreation Centre (DRC), Cotswold District Council (CDC) Planning, CDC Legal and Tetbury Town Council (the Mayor and the Town Clerk).

The purpose of the meeting was to examine some of the Town Council’s concerns over their individual liability and S106 compliance in respect of Project One of the DRC Redevelopment Project.

The summary outcome of the meeting was that:

  • TTC Councillors would hold no personal liability should they vote to release the funds to the DRC (unless they acted way in a manner which was outside of the TTC code of conduct)

  • TTC itself would maintain the legal responsibility and have a duty to demonstrate they worked in a responsible manner to be in the clear.

  • Finally, the planning and legal experts from CDC explicitly confirmed they felt Project One did indeed meet all the requirements of the current S106 funds available.

This outcome is great news as this will now allow TTC to release the S106 funding to the project with the confidence they not only are doing the right thing, but will retain no personal liability, which in turn allows us to crack on with the project.

The matter now needs to be discussed by TTC at a Full Council meeting before the funds can be released. Full Council does not meet again until the end of September, but the Mayor did inform us, just over a week ago, that TTC could, and would, meet before then if needed. For an Extraordinary Meeting of TTC to be held, six councillors need to request it and then a meeting can be held in seven days.

The Town Clerk will be sharing the outcome of today’s meeting with all Councillors and we are hoping that once they receive the information from today’s meeting enough Councillors will understand the need for this project to move forward quickly, given the time limit on £225,000 of GCC funding, and will ask for an extra council session to be held.
