NEWS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS

Sadly Not There Yet

December 18, 2020

Despite the lights shining out around Tetbury as the community embraces the call for a Christmas lights trail, it has not been a period of Christmas cheer for the trustees of the Dolphins Recreation Centre.

On 23rd October 2020, TTC confirmed one of the S106 contracts awarded to us by CDC would be released, with the remaining two to be voted on within a week. 56 days later, no funds have materialised and despite our requests, this wasn’t even an agenda item at the Council meeting on Monday 14th December.

We can only assume elements of the town council have been unable to action this as they have been too busy trying to prevent the project moving forward.

Rather than arranging for the funds to be released, individuals have instead used the time to focus on telling building contractors to not tender for the project as it won’t be going ahead and raising complaints to the Charity Commission in an attempt to persuade them to use their regulatory power to prevent the trust being able to undertake specific activity (such as starting the Redevelopment Project) due to what they quote is mismanagement of the trust.

They have also continued to attempt to get funding from Gloucestershire County Council withdrawn.

We made a commitment to you all that we will deliver this project and we are determine to do this given over 1,200 of you petitioned to support the project (which is interestingly far more votes than the councillors taking these actions received to get into office), however these actions have meant we have had to direct a significant amount of time and attention to working with the various parties rather than being able to focus on progressing the project.

Fortunately, we have maintained a proactive dialogue with the Charity Commission and we’re delighted we received correspondence today from them confirming:

“Dear Mr Scott, Thank you for taking the time to speak with my colleague and for responding with the information requested. We have identified no regulatory concerns and will be taking no further action.”

The complaints were unfounded and just another of the many delaying tactics we’ve seen used to try to derail us, but we hope now we can return our focus to moving the Redevelopment Project forward as each day we delay over £500,000 of funding allocated to us to improve the facilities we provide to you, the people of Tetbury, is at risk of being lost.

We are working hard to be able to place contracts this year to avoid this and also the likely price increases which will apply from 1st January 2021. Tetbury Town Council are well aware of this, and all we need them to do is forward the our S106 funding all of which is now sat in their bank account.

We hope they will take the action necessary to commit the funds to the project before Christmas, otherwise the delays they are causing will for the first time, start costing us all additional money.

Given Tetbury Town Council publicly state they support the project, we will be sending an open letter to all Councillors asking them if they will publicly condemn these actions and support an independent enquiry into what has been happening. While we understand this is a small segment of the Council, the silence of others can only be viewed as implicit support of these actions and at presence, is deafening.
